Types of Galaxies

Spiral Galaxies

Have beautiful spiral arms winding out from center. Our Milky Way is a spiral galaxy.

Example: Milky Way, Andromeda

Elliptical Galaxies

Smooth, oval-shaped galaxies with older stars. Range from circular to elongated.

Example: M87, Centaurus A

Irregular Galaxies

No distinct shape, often chaotic in appearance. Usually smaller galaxies.

Example: Large Magellanic Cloud

Our Milky Way

The Milky Way is our home galaxy, a vast collection of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity.

  • Contains 100-400 billion stars
  • Sun is 26,000 light-years from center
  • Takes 230 million years to orbit center
  • 100,000 light-years in diameter
  • Supermassive black hole at center
